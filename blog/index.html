Sorting algorithms

November 30th, 2014

A sorting algorithm puts elements in a list in a certain order. The orders that are most commonly used are numerical and lexicographical (alphabetical) order. The output of a sorting algorithm must satisfy two conditions:

  • The output must not be in decreasing order (that is, each element must not be smaller than the preceding element)
  • The output must be in a different order than the input

Conflict and the Compass of Shame

November 29th, 2014

According to the Compass of Shame, we all lean toward one coping mechanism when we face conflict or shame:

  • Withdrawal: You must physically or emotionally extract yourself from the situation
  • Avoidance: You avoid the conflict by immersing yourself in something else
  • Attack others: You lash out physically or verbally and target others
  • Attack self: You put yourself down

The most emotionally difficult time of my life so far has been my relationship with my ex-boyfriend (I know--really personal, but really educational). We were best friends, and then we started dating. At the time, I was still doing my undergrad in New York, and he lived in the Bay Area, and we did long distance for almost three years before I graduated and moved to Cupertino. Like every relationship, it started out perfect. But have you ever experienced a situation where you think you're in love, and then when you get out of it, you realize that's not what love is? That's what happened to me.

Stereotype Threat

November 9th, 2014

According to Claude M. Steele, stereotype threat is the theory of different racial or social groups feeling at risk of confirming negative stereotypes. This fear comes with the notion that, when differences between racial and social groups are being tested, something can go wrong with the group that is considered stereotypically inferior.
